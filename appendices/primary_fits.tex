\chapter{Primary \texorpdfstring{\Mbc}{Mbc} fits determining initialisation values}\label{sec:appendix_primary_fits}
Before performing an \Mbc fit of the total data set, the \PDF{s} are defined and pre-fitted on the individual components corresponding to the good tag-\B mesons, combinatorial \BB background and continuum background (see \Cref{sec:fitting_components} for definitions).
The \Mbc fits of each \EB bin (see \Cref{sec:binning}) are provided for the aforementioned components of the data set in this Chapter.
The fitting strategy is introduced in \Cref{sec:fitting_setup}.
The fits are as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Crystal Ball \PDF fits of the good tag-\B meson \Mbc distribution (\Cref{fig:primary_cb_fits}).
    \item ARGUS \PDF fits of the continuum event \Mbc distribution (\Cref{fig:primary_argus_fits}).
    \item Chebyshev \PDF fits of the combinatorial \BB tag background distribution (\Cref{fig:primary_cheb_fits}).
\end{itemize}
Additionally, the ARGUS and Chebyshev \PDF{s} are combined after fitting and a good combined background description is achieved.
This is shown in  \Cref{fig:primary_bkg_fits}.

Although small inaccuracies of the fitter may be spotted (e.g. $2.4-2.6~\gev$ region in \Cref{fig:primary_cheb_fits}) The primary goal of the fitter is to describe the Crystal Ball normalisation parameter.
Moreover, these fits are prepared on 1.6~\invab, whereas the measurement is planned on a data set roughly one order of magnitude smaller.
Therefore, at this stage, such a description performs sufficiently well for the goal of the analysis.
